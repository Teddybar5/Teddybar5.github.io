CurPhoe World Atlas

In the year 2052 the multi-billionaire Shelon Kusk, fictional character :P, launches a space shuttle time capsule into space. This capsule is full of some of the most important inventions throughout human history. The wheel, the printing press, iron, swords, guns, and everything else that was impactful. Along with these phenomenally important inventions he blueprints and parts for a Curtiss P-40E, a Panzer IV tank, and a Leda-Class Frigate were included because Shelon Kusk, eccentric billionaire as he is, insisted that they were necessary for the advancement of mankind and all future alien species. After travelling through space for thousands of years this shuttle crash lands on an alien planet called CurPhoe. Another thousands of years after the technology has dispersed throughout the planet because of the crash landing an intelligent form of life called Frua Toas, a humanoid froglike species, similar to Kua Toas from Dungeons and Dragons, discover the technology. This leads to a thriving nation of Frua Toas who drive tanks and fly WW2 fighter jets. I created an Atlas for this world with some game hooks as well that have promise. I wanted to make a fun world and the idea of frogs flying fighter jets was fun to me.

Draft Chess: Chess Modification

For this project I worked with a group to create a modified version of Chess. I wanted to make a major change but ensure that the change did not ruin the fabric of the game. Ultimately I came up with the idea of placing pieces one at a time during set up. This allows each game to be unique, brings a new element of strategy, but keeps the essence of the game as all pieces still move the same and gameplay is exactly the same.

Play in Education Essays:

I wrote two essays about the impact of Play, specifically playing games and playful attitudes, in education, but more specifically higher education. These are an Context Project essay which explains the need for play in higher education, and an Advocacy Project essay which outlines ways to implement play into higher education. Play is the way that everyones learns outside of school, so why is it not utilized more? This is the question I sought to answer, and I could not find a good reason. Play SHOULD be implemented in every classroom no matter how old the students are.
